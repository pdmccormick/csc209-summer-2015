Labs

"What we have to learn to do, we learn by doing." — Aristotle

Nearly every week you will have a short lab exercise to do. These lab exercises are intended to give you practice with some of concepts covered that week during lecture, and are occasionally designed to help with some part of an assignment.

If you arrive at the lab period having already read through the lab carefully and maybe even attempted the first part of it, you will be able to complete the lab in one hour. It may take longer if you arrive unprepared. The labs will be automatically graded. You are encouraged to work through the exercises with a partner. Labs will be posted at the beginning of the week and due Friday at 10 pm.

There are many reasons to work in pairs. For the purposes of this course, the most important ones are (in decreasing priority):

  • It's more fun!
  • You have someone to talk through each problem with, which often means you can solve a problem faster and get more insight into the problem.
  • You have an opportunity (if you take it) to get to know a few more people in the class.

Attend the lab section to which you are assigned and work with a partner during the hour.

There will be no labs on May 14 (first week of class), June 25 (the week of the midterm) or August 6 (the final week of class.)
